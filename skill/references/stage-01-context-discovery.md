# Stage 1 — Project Context Discovery

## Objective

Understand the environment before discussing requirements. Never start collecting functional requirements before this exists at least in rough form.

## What to discover

- **Organization**: what it does, customers, revenue model, department involved
- **Domain**: industry terminology, regulations, standards, common workflows — build a running glossary as you go
- **Background**: why this project, why now, sponsor, prior attempts, lessons learned
- **Business problem**: symptoms → root cause → business/financial/operational impact (don't stop at symptoms)
- **Current state**: existing workflow, existing systems, bottlenecks, workarounds
- **Desired future state**: expected capabilities, measurable improvements
- **Existing systems**: what's in use, strengths/weaknesses, replace vs. coexist
- **Drivers**: cost reduction, compliance, growth, replacing legacy, customer experience
- **Success criteria**: must be measurable. "Improve UX" is not a success criterion; "cut order processing from 15 min to under 5 min" is.
- **Constraints**: budget, schedule, technology, legal, vendor, contractual
- **Assumptions**: kept separate from facts, each one eventually verified or rejected
- **Risks**: don't try to solve them yet, just capture them
- **Dependencies**: external teams, vendors, third-party systems, approvals
- **Timeline & budget**: fixed or negotiable? what does budget constrain?
- **Technology landscape**: existing platforms/stack — understand, don't yet decide
- **Regulatory environment**: healthcare/finance/privacy/accessibility obligations often become NFRs later

## Discovery strategy

Start broad, narrow based on answers. Each question should close a real gap — don't run through a fixed checklist regardless of what's already been said. If the user gives limited info, follow up; if they give a lot, summarize back and confirm before moving on.

## Output: Project Context Summary

Sections: Organization Overview · Business Domain · Background · Business Problem · Current State · Desired Future State · Goals · Success Criteria · Constraints · Assumptions · Risks · Dependencies · Timeline · Technology Landscape · Regulatory Environment · Outstanding Questions

## Done when

The business problem, objective, and motivation are clear; current and desired future state are both understood; major constraints, assumptions, risks, and dependencies are documented; and remaining unknowns are explicitly listed (not silently dropped). If not, keep discovering before moving to Stage 2.
