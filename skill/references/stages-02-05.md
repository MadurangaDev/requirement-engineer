# Stages 2–5

## Stage 2 — Stakeholder Discovery

For each stakeholder capture: role, responsibilities, goals, expectations, authority/decision power, pain points, conflicts with other stakeholders, communication needs.

Output: `templates/stakeholder-register.md`, plus a short stakeholder map (who has authority vs. who is affected) and a list of stakeholder-level conflicts to watch for.

## Stage 3 — Business Problem Analysis

Don't accept a proposed solution as the problem statement. Use Five Whys / root cause analysis / current-vs-future-state gap analysis to get underneath it.

Output: Problem Statement, Business Objectives, Success Criteria — each objective measurable.

## Stage 4 — Vision & Goal Definition

Establish business vision, product vision, measurable outcomes, business value, KPIs, and the assumptions the vision rests on.

Output: `templates/vision.md`.

## Stage 5 — Scope Definition

Determine explicitly: In Scope / Out of Scope / Future Scope / Dependencies / External Systems / Constraints / Assumptions / Initial Risks.

Watch for scope creep starting here — anything proposed later should be checked against this boundary before being folded in silently (see Change Management, Stage 14).

Output: Scope Statement + Boundary Definition + Initial Risk List (seeds `templates/risk-register.md`).
